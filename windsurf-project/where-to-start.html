BEFORE YOU START, THERE ARE SOME TERMS YOU NEED TO KNOW

Please note, this page only lists games that are considered good for beginners to start with. This is not a list of all the games. This will also not touch on the extra stages, and this is through the lens of a new player attempting a normal 1CC run.

PC-98 Era

▼

The oldest and most primitive games in the series. These are not recommended for beginners, and most have not aged well. They are very great games to explore after playing all the Windows games however! If you want to learn more about this era, the List of Games section has lots of info about it!

Early Windows Era

▼

The Early Windows era (2002-2005) marked the transition to the games being developed and released for the Windows operating system. This early era established many of the core gameplay mechanics that continue to be seen in modern Touhou games.

Embodiment of Scarlet Devil (2002)

The very first Windows game! This game is hard. Especially for a new player. It is more similar to the PC-98 games and does not have any mechanics to help you survive, with the exception of having the ability to adjust your starting lives and bombs. However, if you are able to get through this game as a new player, you should be able to handle anything the later games throw at you! For now...

Perfect Cherry Blossom (2003)

One of, if not the easiest Touhou games and is highly recommended for beginners! The bullet-hell (further on referred to as "Danmaku") patterns are also some of the easiest in the series. Youmu and Yuyuko can make tough bosses, but the game gives you a "Supernatural Border!" which essentially acts as a shield. The shot types in this game are very powerful as well.

Imperishable Night (2004)

One of the most beloved games in the series! Imperishable Night, not only filled with content, is also one of the most beginner friendly. While it has very complex mechanics, such as the time mechanic, Youkai gauge, team system and more, the patterns themselves are not too difficult for beginners, and the game gives you many mechanics to help you survive.

Middle Windows Era

▼

The Middle Windows era (2007-2013) saw a significant overhaul of many mechanics and gameplay aspects the series has had thus far.

Mountain of Faith (2007)

Mountain of Faith marks a sort of reboot for the series as EoSD had, with future games more similar to this one. This is yet another beginner friendly game, with the danmaku patterns fairly simple, even on harder diffilculties. This and Subterranean Animism have a new mechanic for bombs. They are now tied to your power level, with your power decreasing by one if a bomb is used. This game also introduces a new continue system used from here up to until Ten Desires. You have infinite continues, but you must restart the stage you lost on from the beginning. However, Mountain of Faith still provides lots of resources and mechanics to aid survival.

Ten Desires (2011)

Ten Desires introduces the Spirit Point and Trance mechanics, adding new layers to the gameplay. Ten Desires has the easiest danmaku patterns of any game so far. However, it is very scarce on resources, such as lives and bombs, as you are expected to make use of the new mechanics. Ten Desires brings back the usual bomb and limited continue systems games before Mountain of Faith had, which depending on the player, can be upside or downside.

Current Windows Era

▼

The Current Windows era (2015-present) represents the most recent entries in the series. As Touhou is still an on-going series, this section has the potential to become outdated in the near future.

Hidden Star in Four Seasons (2017)

This game introduces the Seasons mechanic. Allowing you to choose from Spring, Summer, Fall, and Winter. Each season giving you various abilities. While this is not one of the easiest Touhou games, with a significant difficulty spike at Stage four and five, the seasons options and season release mechanic provide enough to survive the danmaku. However, this description is very simplified, so you are encouraged to discover how the mechanics work yourself!

Wily Beast and Weakest Creature (2019)

This game introduces the animal spirit system! While choosing a character, you are given the option between Wolf, Otter, or Eagle, each having a respective effect on your focused shot, bomb, or unfocused shot. This game has a similar mechanic to UNdefined Fantastic Object. You collet animal spirits, usually appearing after defeating an enemy. Collecting a certain amount will cause your attack to become hyper-powerful! Or in the case of the otter spirit, mostly shielding you. Wily Beast and Weakest Creature has fairly simple danmaku patterns, but is far from one of the easiest Touhou games.
