Official vs Fan-made Content

A lot of Touhou's appeal is its many, extensive fan works created across decades! While this is amazing, this also comes with the downside of it being difficult to know what is official and what is not.

A lot of confusion comes from Touhou music. Official music is heard in-game and from ZUN's albums. Here, we will use an example from Hifuu Club, a separate album-based side story connected to Touhou. You can tell this is official due to ZUN's art style and sound of the album, being completely instrumental.

Official Hifuu Club album by ZUN. Can be listened to here.

An arrangement album by doujin rock band Sekkenya, using music from Hifuu Club as a basis. Can be listened to here. Spotify link due to region locking on YouTube Music.

Did you know that the song playing on this page right now, assuming you have audio enabled, is from a doujin arrangement album?

While this is a good rule of thumb to follow, ZUN often endorses or officially licenses Touhou works. Touhou Lost Word, a mobile game, is officially licensed by ZUN, or in this case, Team Shanghai Alice. ZUN however, often has little to no involvement in these works.

Official Touhou art is also fairly easy to spot. In this case, official art will refer to art completely made by ZUN, such as in Touhou games. ZUN has a distinct art style that many artists have not fully replicated yet. For example, did you know the Reimu and Marisa sprites in the main page of this website are not official, and are from the fan-game, "Puppet Dance Performance"? The background of the main page is also unofficial, using art from Touhou New World.
